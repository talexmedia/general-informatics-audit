01 The Five Dimensions

The Foundation: How General Informatics Reads On Our Audit Lens

Five foundational dimensions, applied to your public presence from the outside. These are the layer every piece of content has to satisfy before craft becomes a multiplier.

01
Audience Clarity Partial
Your content speaks to two different communities at once. The Grind targets "people grinding to execute at a higher level," the Donald Monistere personal-brand community. geninf.com speaks to procurement at banks, schools, and maritime orgs. Both are valid. The relationship between them is not yet articulated, so a viewer who finds one has no clear path to the other.
02
Authenticity & Voice Partial
The Grind shows your real voice: unscripted, opinion-first, coming from a three-book foundation. That is the strongest version of you publicly available. The gap is the LinkedIn About, all-caps executive-bio copy, and geninf.com, where the voice defaults to vendor. The most authentic surface is the show that stopped publishing in November 2023.
03
Storytelling Power Partial
Three named customer stories on the homepage (Celtic Group, LeBlanc Pediatrics, Hissho Sushi) with specific outcomes. Debbie's quote is pure story in four sentences. Two of those stories already exist as films on your YouTube channel: LeBlanc Pediatrics ("More than A Phone Number," May 2024) and Tacala (May 2024). The story investment is made. The medium is served. What's missing is the craft lift and the placement that puts each film where the prospect actually meets it.
04
Generosity & Value Strong
Three books. Two seasons of The Grind. 57 videos on YouTube including customer films for LeBlanc Pediatrics, Tacala, FC Dallas, and Volunteers of America. A weekly blog with named authors from across the team. "How IT Works" as a separate educational series. This is a content brand by MSP standards. Your strongest dimension, and the one every part of this audit builds from.
05
Consistency & Systems Critical
The blog is consistent. YouTube has also held a roughly monthly rhythm. The Grind as a show stopped in November 2023. Standalone uploads took over, which is not the same as a franchise viewers return to. X went dark September 17, 2025. Seven months of silence on a 17-year-old account. The IMS Solutions Group acquisition (April 10, 2026) is a once-a-decade event and has a press release and a blog post, no film. Consistency of publishing exists. Consistency of format and brand does not.
02 Quality

After The Foundation, The Craft

Most brands get the sequence wrong. They invest in production before they've earned the right to be heard. Beautiful content built on nothing.

But there is another version of this story. You do the foundational work first. You build the generosity, the voice, the real stories. You earn the trust. And once all of that is genuinely in place, broadcast-quality production stops being a luxury and becomes a multiplier.

It signals to the people already paying attention that you take them seriously enough to match the care they've invested in you with an equal investment in the video content you deliver to them. That is when production value stops being just cosmetic and starts making a difference.

03 Video Ideas

5 Ideas For Video Content

Five concepts based on what General Informatics already has, or could easily create. Different formats. Different jobs. Same craft standard.

1
"More Than A Phone Number, Directed"
The customer hero film. Built from the existing May 2024 Debbie / LeBlanc Pediatrics interview plus a return visit eighteen months later. Four acts, broadcast standard, six to eight minutes. Positioned as the anchor video on geninf.com, the one every sales conversation and case study page points back to.

2
"The Grind, Season 3"
Restart the show that drove your strongest content from 2017 to 2023. A named, episodic format with a release calendar viewers can trust. Twelve episodes, batched shoot days, broadcast-grade production. The IMS Solutions Group acquisition becomes Episode 2: "Why AI, Why Now, What Changes For Our Clients." Each long-form episode is built to cut down into shorter pieces.
3
"How IT Works, Reborn"
Standalone explainer series, ninety seconds to three minutes per episode. Each piece answers a specific buyer question: what is XDR, what does an MSP actually do, what does HIPAA-compliant IT really mean. Hosted by named authors across the team (Conner LeBlanc, Chris Campbell, Aaron Lancaster). Built to be repurposed into shorter clips and embedded directly on the related itstrategy.tech blog post.
4
"The Acquisition Film"
A six to eight minute documentary on the IMS Solutions Group acquisition. The biggest news event the company has had in years currently has a press release and a blog post. The film captures Donald, IMS leadership, and named customers reacting in real time. The kind of once-a-decade story that lives longer when it's properly told.
5
"Enhanced, On Camera"
Adapt your Enhanced trilogy into a serialized lecture series. One five to seven minute episode per principle, in a conversational format that draws on customer examples to illustrate. Three books worth of material translated into the medium where it lands hardest. Cuts down into a library of standalone short-form pieces.
04 Idea 1, In Depth

A Video We'd Make For General Informatics

The full version of idea 1 from above, designed end to end.

Working Title · Director's Cut of an Existing Film
More Than A Phone Number, Directed
"You already shot Debbie's story. Seventy-nine people have seen it. Here's what it becomes when we direct it, and get it in front of the community you've already built."
Format
We build the film from the existing May 2024 footage and add a fresh A-camera day with Debbie eighteen months on. The cut goes through a broadcast sound mix and color grade, finishing at six to eight minutes. The finished film becomes the anchor video on geninf.com, the piece every sales conversation, outbound email, and case study page points to.
Act I · What The Line Meant In 2024
We open with selected footage from the original film, recut. Debbie retells what was happening the day she first said the line: what broke and who picked up. This footage sets up the promise that the rest of the film will test.
Act II · What It Costs To Earn It
Donald addresses any IT buyer watching. He walks through the three operational decisions General Informatics made in 2023 that made it possible for Debbie to say what she said in 2024. These are the choices behind the kind of trust her quote captures.
Act III · The Return Visit
We return to Debbie in her clinic on a normal Tuesday. There is no crisis. Her team is larger, her practice has grown, and the relationship with General Informatics has held. This is the quiet evidence that the original promise survived its follow-up visit. The scene cannot be faked because it only exists if the story was true to begin with.
Act IV · The Test
The film closes with one transferable lesson. Donald presents a three-question diagnostic the viewer can take into their next vendor meeting, and the questions stay vendor-agnostic. His closing line is not a sales pitch but an invitation to hold every partner, General Informatics included, to the same standard.
Where It Lives
geninf.com hero, replacing the static hero image as the new anchor video · pinned to the top of youtube.com/@generalinformatics so every first visitor lands on the best work · embedded on the "More Than Just a Phone Number" case study page · clipdowns for LinkedIn, Facebook, and X · reshared from Donald's personal LinkedIn · a chapter link in the next itstrategy.tech essay.
What The Viewer Gets
The viewer gets two things they can use right away. First, they see evidence that a real customer's promise survived its eighteen-month follow-up visit. Second, they get a vendor-agnostic diagnostic they can take into any IT partner conversation, given freely with no obligation. Six to eight minutes of film replace a pitch deck, and by the end the viewer has a sharper question to ask the next vendor across the table, whether that vendor is General Informatics or not.
